# Cover Letter Draft for Journal of Agricultural Engineering

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript entitled "A Quality-Controlled Semi-Supervised Detection Framework for Cross-Domain Oil Palm Fresh Fruit Bunch Maturity Grading" for consideration as an Original Article in the Journal of Agricultural Engineering.

This manuscript addresses a practical agricultural engineering problem: oil palm fresh fruit bunch maturity detectors trained on same-source video-frame datasets can show strong apparent performance but fail when evaluated on a different dataset or acquisition domain. To address this issue, the study develops and evaluates a quality-controlled semi-supervised YOLOv8 framework with scene-disjoint source-domain evaluation, locked external-domain testing, class-space harmonization, four-class masked external evaluation, and precision-first pseudo-label filtering.

The work is aligned with the scope of the Journal of Agricultural Engineering because it focuses on a field-oriented decision-support pipeline for crop maturity grading rather than only a generic computer vision benchmark. The study reports three-seed formal experiments and distinguishes clearly between source-only transfer, target-domain adaptation, and strict semi-supervised self-training. The proposed detector is framed as a human-in-the-loop quality-control aid for collection-point, grading-line, or mobile inspection use, not as a complete replacement for trained graders. This conservative interpretation avoids overstating the contribution and provides practical guidance for agricultural deployment.

The manuscript has not been published previously and is not under consideration by another journal. The author declares no conflict of interest. The code, configuration templates, manuscript files, and selected evaluation reports are available in the public project repository, while raw third-party datasets and trained weights are not redistributed because they depend on the original dataset licenses and access conditions.

Thank you for considering this manuscript. I look forward to your response.
